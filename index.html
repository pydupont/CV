About Me

My research focuses on the association between the fungal endophyte Epichloe festucae and perennial ryegrass, using bioinformatics and next generation sequencing approaches. The association between fungal endophytes and grasses is used for forage improvement. Indeed, the fungus protects its host against a wide range of pests (insects and pathogenic fungi) as well as against drought.

My postdoctoral research program comprises three main areas of research: the study of lateral gene transfers between plant-associated fungi, the host transcriptomic response to endophyte infection and the comparison of mutualistic and pathogenic associations between fungi and their host plants. The study of Lateral gene transfer between plant-associated fungi (pathogens, endophytes, mycorrhiza) is based on the genomic comparison of different fungi and started with the annotation of the gene models of Epichloe festucae and its host, perrenial ryegrass. My work on the host responses to endophyte infection was performed on a transcriptomic level and a transcriptomic and metabolomic signature of the infection was described. The comparison of mutualistic and pathogenic interaction was performed on different Epichloe strains. Some Epichloe festucae mutants show a switch from a mutualistic behavior to pathogen-like. A transcriptomic study comparing three of these mutants to the original wild-type association was performed. This comparison led to the discovery of a set of fungal genes involved in symbiosis.

Software and Databases

Epichloe gene models database

This database is a Ruby on Rails web application linked to a MySQL server. It contains the results of automatic annotations for 9000 Epichloe festucae genes models as well as transcriptomics results and manual annotations. It is used both by national and international researchers. It was publically announced in Eaton et al. (2015).

Perennial Ryegrass gene models database

This database is based on the same framework as the Epichloe festucae database and contains automatic annotations of about 50.000 gene models. It was publically announced in Dupont et al. (2015).

Hybrid Lineage Transcriptome Explorer (HyLiTE)

HyLiTE is a software allowing the analysis of high-throughput transcriptome data from allopolyploid species
