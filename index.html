Pierre-Yves Dupont

I am a postdoctoral researcher in computational biology working at Massey University - New Zealand

About Me

My research focuses on the association between the fungal endophyte Epichloe festucae and perennial ryegrass, using bioinformatics and next generation sequencing approaches. The association between fungal endophyte and grasses is used for forage improvment. Indeed, the fungus protects its host against a wide range of pests (insects and pathogenic fungi) as well as against drought.

My postdoctoral research program comprises three main areas of research:

•   Lateral gene transfer between plant-associated fungi (pathogens, endophytes, mycorrhiza). This work is based on the genomic comparison of different fungi and started with the annotation of Epichloe festucae gene models and its host, perrenial ryegrass

•   Host responses to endophyte infection. In parallel with the genomic analyses, transcriptomics studies were performed to explore the transcriptomics response of the host to its symbiont.

•   Comparison of mutualism and pathogenism. Some Epichloe festucae mutants show a switch from a mutualistic behavior to pathogenic. A transcriptomic study comparing three of these mutant to the original wild-type association was performed. This comparison lead us to the discovery of a set of fungal genes involved in symbiosis

Databases

Epichloe gene models database • March 2007 - February 2010

This database is a Ruby on Rails web application linked to a MySQL server. It contains the results of automatic annotations for 9000 Epichloe festucae genes models as well as transcriptomics results and manual annotations. It is used by people in Massey University and in the Noble Fundation (USA). It has been publically announced in Eaton et al. (2015).

Perennial Ryegrass gene models database • March 2007 - February 2010

This database is based on the same framework as the Epichloe festucae and contains the automatic annotations of about 50.000 gene models. It has been publically announced in Dupont PY et al. (2015).
